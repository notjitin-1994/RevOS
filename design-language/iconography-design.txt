Icon Vocabulary (The Metaphors)In a garage context, we need specific mapping to ensure specific technical concepts are represented accurately. We avoid "cartoony" metaphors.2.1 Core NavigationConceptIcon Name (Lucide)ReasoningDashboard<LayoutGrid /> or <Gauge />Represents the "Control Center."Jobs/Repairs<Wrench />The universal symbol for active mechanical work.Schedule/Bay<CalendarRange />Represents time-blocking for lifts/bays.Inventory<PackageSearch />Represents tracking parts (not just a box).Customers<Users />Standard CRM representation.Invoices<FileText />Represents the finalized document.Settings<Settings2 />Important: Use Settings2 (sliders) instead of Settings (gear) to differentiate from "Mechanical Parts."2.2 Automotive SpecificsConceptIcon Name (Lucide)ReasoningVehicle<CarFront />Looks technical/blueprint-like.Engine/Motor<Cpu /> or <Zap />Represents modern diagnostics/ECU (Engine Control Unit).Diagnostics<Activity />Represents live data streams/pulse.VIN Number<Barcode />Represents the unique ID scan.Tires/Wheels<CircleDashed />Abstract representation of a wheel/rotation.Fluids/Oil<Droplet />Universal symbol for liquid inputs.3. Accessibility & UX Directives5.1 Decorative IconsIf an icon is purely visual (e.g., next to a label "Settings"), it must be hidden from screen readers to avoid clutter.Code: <Settings2 className="w-4 h-4" aria-hidden="true" />5.2 Standalone IconsIf an icon is the button (e.g., a trash can with no text), it MUST have a label.Code:JavaScript<button aria-label="Delete Invoice">
  <Trash2 className="w-4 h-4 text-status-error" />
</button>
5.3 Click TargetsIcons are small (20px), but fingers are big. Never put an onClick directly on the icon SVG. Put it on the parent <button> or <div> and ensure that parent has padding (p-2) to create a minimum 44x44px touch target.
